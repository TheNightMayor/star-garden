# Time of Uncertainty
## 67-168 AG
### Stardust Plague
The Stardust Plague was a terrible pathogen that broke out in the Pact Worlds in 67 AG and ended up killing millions of individuals.1 Its devastation was thought to have been brought home and spread by unchecked exploration of the galaxy through the (at the time) recently discovered means of Drift travel 

a popular conspiracy theory holds rogue Xenowardens responsible for the Stardust Plague—a deadly pathogen brought back by early interstellar explorers, supposedly as part of a plot to discourage interstellar colonization

Once used as quarantine sections during the great Stardust Plague, many of the sections of the Arms on Absalom Station have been converted into affordable housing
## Time of Uncertainty
during the Stardust Plague, public opinion turned against the Society, beginning a century-long “Time of Uncertainty.” During these tumultuous years, First Seeker Hilbrin expanded the Lorespire Complex and acquired the technology required to create Guidance, and First Seeker Lanrah promised to solve the Gap. Lanrah’s tenure was so disastrous, future First Seekers were forever banned from choosing Gap-related mandates. 
## 160-168 AG
### Lezosk's Perplexity Sealed 
After Lanrah died during the Battle of the Amethyst Transcendence in 160 AG, her successor, First Seeker Lezosk, spent his term building a secret training ground known as “Lezosk’s Perplexity.” The Perplexity was sealed after Lezosk’s mysterious disappearance in 168 AG—the doors open once every year, and elite Starfinders are dispatched on missions within. With Lezosk’s disappearance, the Time of Uncertainty came to an end and the Society rebounded, entering a long period of prosperity and stability.
